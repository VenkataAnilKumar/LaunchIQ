# User Research Summary
## LaunchIQ — AI-Powered Product Launch Intelligence Platform

**Version:** 1.0
**Author:** Venkata Anil Kumar
**Date:** 2026-04-09

---

## 1. Research Goals

- Understand the current product launch process for marketers and founders
- Identify the biggest pain points and time sinks in launch planning
- Validate demand for an AI-native launch intelligence tool
- Discover what features would drive immediate adoption

---

## 2. Methodology

| Method | Participants | Duration |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| 1:1 Discovery Interviews | 12 marketers & founders | 30–45 min each |
| Online Survey | 85 respondents | 5–7 min |
| Competitive Product Usability Review | 4 tools | Desk research |
| Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) Analysis | Derived from interviews | — |

**Participant Mix:**
- Startup founders (35%)
- Product marketers at B2B SaaS (40%)
- Growth marketers at scale-ups (25%)

---

## 3. Key User Personas

### Persona 1 — "The Overwhelmed Founder"
- Solo or 2-person team
- No dedicated marketing hire
- Spends 15–20 hrs/week on launch research and planning
- Frustrated by scattered tools and no clear launch framework
- **JTBD:** "Help me launch like I have a marketing team."

### Persona 2 — "The Stretched Product Marketer"
- Works at 50–200 person B2B SaaS company
- Manages 3–5 product launches per year
- Relies on manual research, spreadsheets, and slide decks
- **JTBD:** "Give me launch intelligence so I can focus on strategy, not data gathering."

### Persona 3 — "The Growth Hacker"
- Fast-moving, experiment-driven
- Wants AI suggestions for messaging tests and channel prioritization
- **JTBD:** "Tell me what's working in the market so I can move faster than competitors."

## 4. Top Pain Points Discovered

| # | Pain Point | % Respondents |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | Too much time spent on manual competitor research | 78% |
| 2 | No clear framework for structuring a launch plan | 71% |
| 3 | Disconnected tools — no single launch view | 68% |
| 4 | Writing launch copy is slow and repetitive | 65% |
| 5 | Hard to know if messaging resonates before launch | 59% |
| 6 | Launch post-mortems rarely happen — no learning loop | 52% |

---

## 5. Key Insights

**Insight 1 — Launch planning is still largely manual**
> "I spend 2 weeks just researching before I write a single word of strategy." — Product Marketer, Series A SaaS

**Insight 2 — Marketers want a co-pilot, not full automation**
> Most users (74%) want AI to do the research and drafting but want to approve final strategy decisions.

**Insight 3 — Speed is the primary value driver**
> The #1 desired outcome: compress launch prep from weeks to days without sacrificing quality.

**Insight 4 — Content generation is a secondary but strong hook**
> Once the strategy exists, 82% of users want AI to generate launch copy automatically.

**Insight 5 — Integrations matter at adoption, not evaluation**
> Users evaluate on intelligence quality first; integrations (HubSpot, Slack) are a retention driver, not an acquisition driver.

---

## 6. Implications for Product Design

| Insight | Design Implication |
|---------|-------------------|
| Manual research dominates time | Market Intelligence Agent must be the #1 wow moment |
| Want co-pilot not autopilot | Build HITL checkpoints at every major agent output |
| Speed is primary value | Onboarding must deliver first launch brief in < 10 minutes |
| Content gen is secondary hook | Content Agent should activate after strategy is approved |
| Integrations are retention drivers | Build Slack + HubSpot integrations before Series A |
