# Product Requirements Document (PRD)
## LaunchIQ — AI-Powered Product Launch Intelligence Platform

**Version:** 1.0
**Author:** Venkata Anil Kumar
**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Status:** Draft

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Marketing teams and founders spend weeks manually researching competitors, defining audiences, planning launch campaigns, and coordinating execution — only to miss the optimal launch window or misalign messaging with market reality. There is no intelligent, unified system that automates launch intelligence end-to-end.

---

## 2. Product Vision

LaunchIQ is an AI-native, multi-agent launch intelligence platform that autonomously researches markets, generates launch strategies, creates campaign content, and iterates based on real-time performance — so marketers can launch faster, smarter, and with more confidence.

---

## 3. Target Users

| Persona | Role | Pain Point |
|---------|------|------------|
| Startup Founder | Solo or early-stage | No marketing team, overwhelmed by launch complexity |
| Product Marketer | B2B SaaS company | Manual research, slow strategy cycles |
| Growth Marketer | Scale-up | Disconnected tools, no unified launch view |
| Marketing Manager | Mid-market | Coordinating multiple vendors and tools |

---

## 4. Goals & Success Metrics

### Product Goals
- Reduce time-to-launch-strategy from weeks to hours
- Automate 70%+ of launch research and content generation
- Deliver measurable improvement in launch campaign performance

### Success Metrics (MVP)
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Time to first launch plan | < 10 minutes |
| User activation rate | > 60% within 7 days |
| Weekly active usage | > 40% of registered users |
| NPS Score | > 40 |
| Agent task completion rate | > 90% |

---

## 5. User Stories

**As a startup founder, I want to:**
- Input my product description and get a full launch strategy in minutes
- See competitor positioning so I can differentiate my messaging
- Generate ready-to-use launch copy for email, social, and ads

**As a product marketer, I want to:**
- Get AI-generated audience segments with messaging recommendations
- Create a phased launch roadmap with milestones automatically
- Track launch performance and receive optimization suggestions

**As a growth marketer, I want to:**
- Run A/B messaging experiments suggested by AI
- Integrate with my existing tools (HubSpot, Slack, GA4)
- See a unified launch dashboard across all channels

---

## 6. Feature List (MVP Scope)

### Core Features
1. **Launch Intelligence Brief** — AI-generated market, competitor, and audience summary
2. **Launch Strategy Generator** — Phased go-to-market plan with milestones
3. **Audience Persona Builder** — AI-defined buyer personas with messaging angles
4. **Competitive Positioning Map** — Visual differentiation matrix
5. **Campaign Content Generator** — Email sequences, social posts, ad copy
6. **Launch Checklist & Tracker** — Step-by-step execution tracker
7. **Performance Analytics Dashboard** — Real-time launch metrics and AI recommendations

### Agentic Features (MVP)
- Orchestrator Agent coordinating all sub-agents
- Market Intelligence Agent (automated research)
- Content Generation Agent (multi-format output)
- Human-in-the-Loop approval checkpoints

---

## 7. Out of Scope (MVP)

- Native mobile app
- Direct ad platform integrations (Phase 2)
- Custom model fine-tuning
- White-label / reseller version
- Real-time collaboration (multi-user simultaneous editing)

---

## 8. Assumptions

- Users have a product concept or existing product to launch
- Primary market: B2B SaaS and tech products
- Internet connectivity required (cloud-based agents)
- Claude API available and within acceptable cost range
- English language only for MVP

---

## 9. Constraints

- MVP budget: bootstrapped / minimal cloud costs
- Team: solo founder
- Timeline: 8–12 weeks to working prototype
- Compliance: GDPR-aware data handling from day one

---

## 10. Open Questions

1. Should LaunchIQ support physical product launches (e-commerce) in Phase 1?
2. What is the right pricing model — seat-based, usage-based, or outcome-based?
3. Should the platform support team collaboration in MVP or Phase 2?
4. Which integrations are highest priority for early adopters?
5. How much human-in-the-loop is optimal before users feel the AI is too autonomous?
